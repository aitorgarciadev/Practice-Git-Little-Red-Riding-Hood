Little Red Riding Hood

Chapter 1: The Red Cloak

Once upon a time, in a quiet village surrounded by deep, dark forests, there lived a cheerful young girl named Little Red Riding Hood. She earned her name from the bright red cloak her grandmother had made for her. Little Red Riding Hood was loved by everyone who knew her, especially her grandmother, who lived on the other side of the forest.

Chapter 2: A Special Task

One sunny morning, Little Red Riding Hood's mother called her into the kitchen. "Your grandmother is not feeling well," she said, handing her a basket filled with fresh bread, butter, and sweet treats. "Take this basket to her, but remember, stay on the path and do not talk to strangers." Little Red Riding Hood promised to follow her mother's advice and set off for her grandmother's house.

Chapter 3: Into the Woods

As Little Red Riding Hood ventured deeper into the forest, she admired the vibrant flowers and listened to the birds singing. Suddenly, a large wolf appeared from behind a tree. "Good morning, Little Red Riding Hood," said the wolf, trying to hide his hunger behind a friendly smile. "Where are you going on this fine day?" Little Red Riding Hood, forgetting her mother's warning, replied, "I am going to visit my grandmother who lives at the end of this path."

Chapter 4: A Wicked Plan

The wolf's eyes gleamed with cunning. "What a lovely idea," he said. "But why not bring her some flowers too? There are some beautiful ones just off the path." Little Red Riding Hood, charmed by the wolf's suggestion, wandered into the woods to pick flowers. The wolf, meanwhile, hurried to the grandmother's house with a devious plan in mind.

Chapter 5: The Grandmother's House

The wolf arrived at the grandmother's house and knocked on the door. "Who is it?" called the grandmother. "It's me, Little Red Riding Hood," replied the wolf, disguising his voice. The kind grandmother, thinking it was her beloved granddaughter, unlocked the door. The wolf leapt inside and gobbled her up in a single bite. Then he put on her clothes and climbed into bed, waiting for Little Red Riding Hood.

Chapter 6: The Big Bad Wolf

Little Red Riding Hood finally arrived at her grandmother's house, her basket now filled with flowers as well. She knocked on the door. "Who is it?" came the gruff voice of the wolf. "It's me, Little Red Riding Hood," she answered. "Come in, dear," the wolf called out. Little Red Riding Hood entered and approached the bed. "Grandmother, what big ears you have!" she exclaimed. "All the better to hear you with, my dear," replied the wolf. "Grandmother, what big eyes you have!" "All the better to see you with, my dear." "Grandmother, what big hands you have!" "All the better to hug you with, my dear." "But Grandmother, what big teeth you have!" "All the better to eat you with!" roared the wolf, jumping out of the bed.

Chapter 7: A Hero Appears

Just as the wolf was about to pounce on Little Red Riding Hood, a woodcutter who had been passing by heard the commotion. He burst into the house and saw the wolf ready to devour the little girl. With a swift swing of his axe, he struck the wolf down and rescued Little Red Riding Hood.
